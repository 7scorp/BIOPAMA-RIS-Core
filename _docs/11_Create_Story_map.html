How to develop your story

Reflect on the following points before starting:

Tips

  • Target audience: is it scientific, political, or technical ?
  • Terminology: is your target audience familiar with the terms you are using?
  • Purpose: what is the ultimate message behind this story?
  • The 'So what?': how your data and information can lead (or have led) to better decisions?

'The 4 Cs'

  • Contents: do you have the right to use all maps, legends, data, photos, information, etc.?
  • Credits: be sure that copyright for the above elements are addressed appropriately.
  • Consistency: use the same format all along the story (e.g. always include map legends in the same space).
  • Congruence: use paragraphs and sub-paragraphs to talk about topics that are connected. (e.g. SPECIES - Elephants, Rhinos, etc.).

Structure

  • Introduction: what is this story about?
  • Relevance: why this topic is important? Why did you collect d
  • Relevance: why this topic is important? Why did you collect data and infata and information on that?
  • Key messages: how these data and information contribute to improve the current situation?
  • Conclusion: solution, best practice, achievement, or biodiversity target achieved.

Breaking banner A sentence can introduce the story. This story is about human pressure on land in Tanzania.
General Narrow down the attention on one of your topic, e.g. introduce the country/protected area(s). Provide general information. Tanzania one of the largest countries of East Africa. A large share of the population relies on subsistence farming for living.

Where to publish your story

Pubish your story on the 'Stories section' of the RIS (https://rris.biopama.org/biopama_stories) and in biopama.org (link)

PS don't forget to log in to access both systems.

How to edit your story (for developers only)

Your story is in a JSON or HTML file. Both can be edited manually. You can store a backup file of it for your own convenience,too.

If you created it in ArcGIS, go to ArcGIS online assistant to access your HTML or JSON file. Follow the instructions to make editings and savings there.
